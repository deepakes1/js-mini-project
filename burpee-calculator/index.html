How do you do a burpee exercise:

  1. Stand straight, with your legs about shoulder-width apart.
  2. Lower yourself to a squat, and place your hands on the floor
  3. Kick your feet back vigorously to a plank. Your body should keep a straight line - do not stick your butt in the air or lower your hips to the floor.
  4. Do a push-up!
  5. Jump the feet back to the starting position.
  6. Jump explosively in the air.

Using the burpee calorie calculator

To use our burpee calorie calculator, you need to provide only two parameters: the number of burpees performed and your actual weight. Input both numbers into the calculator. While typing in the weight, remember that you can play with the units as much as you need.

The calculator will give you a result as a range of the burpee calories burned, together with a mean value. Being on the lower or the higher side of the range depends primarily on:

  • The pace of the exercise (doing it as fast as you can versus taking it slowly); and
  • The effort you put into the move (e.g., classic push-up versus knee push-up).

Beginner burpees, half burpees, squat thrusts

Suppose you're only starting your fitness journey and not feeling up to a regular burpee. In that case, you can try a half burpee/beginner burpee/squat thrust. Those are the names of the exercises that cut out the push-up, and sometimes a jump, from a burpee.
